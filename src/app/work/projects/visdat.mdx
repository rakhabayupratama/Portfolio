## Overview

This collection showcases two data visualization dashboards developed using **Tableau** as part of academic coursework. Each dashboard was designed to transform complex datasets into clear, interactive visual narratives, making data accessible and actionable for a non-technical audience.

---

## Project 1 (Mid-Semester) - Amazon Sales in India

### Background

This project explores **Amazon's sales performance in India**, analyzing product categories, order statuses, regional distribution, and weekly revenue trends. The dashboard was designed to give a comprehensive snapshot of e-commerce activity and consumer behavior patterns.

### Dataset

- **Source:** [Amazon Sales Dataset - Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/thedevastator/unlock-profits-with-e-commerce-sales-data/data)
- **Scope:** Orders across multiple product categories, states, and size variants
- **Period:** Weekly snapshot (March 31 - April 6, 2022)

### Key Visualizations

- **Bar Chart (Product by Category)** - Breakdown of product quantities across categories: Set (30,819), Kurta (30,653), Top (7,488), Western Dress (7,152), Ethnic Dress (754), Blouse (622), Bottom (251), and Saree (248)
- **Line Chart (Weekly Revenue)** - Total sales value in Indian Rupees over 7 days, peaking at ₹566,777 on April 6, 2022 with a combined weekly total reaching over ₹2.3 million
- **Map (Qty by State)** - Geographic distribution of orders across Indian states, visualizing regional demand concentration
- **Bar Chart (Size by Category)** - Product size distribution (XS to 6XL) segmented by category, with size M and L being most popular
- **Donut Chart (Courier Status)** - 94.35% of orders were Shipped, with 5.65% Unshipped
- **Business Customer Filter** - Toggle between regular (115,614 orders) and business customers (840 orders)

### Key Findings

- **Set** and **Kurta** dominate sales volume, together accounting for over 80% of all products sold
- Weekly revenue peaked on **April 6** (₹566,777), suggesting strong end-of-week purchasing behavior
- The overwhelming majority of orders (**94.35%**) were successfully shipped, indicating a healthy fulfillment rate
- Business customers represent a very small segment (840 vs 115,614), suggesting Amazon India's primary market is individual consumers
- Size **M and L** are the most ordered across almost all clothing categories

### Tools Used

- **Tableau** - full dashboard development including filters, maps, and all chart types
- **Microsoft Excel** - initial data exploration and formatting

## Project 2 (Final Project) - Performance of E-Government in Pacific Nations (2010-2020)

### Background

This project analyzes the **E-Government Development Index (EGDI)** across 12 Pacific Island nations over a decade (2010-2020). The goal was to identify trends, compare national performances, and understand how digital government services have evolved in the Pacific region.

### Dataset

- **Source:** [E-Government Development Index - Pacific Data Hub](https://pacificdata.org/data/dataset/e-government-development-index-df-egov)
- **Scope:** 12 Pacific Island Countries - Fiji, Tonga, Samoa, Palau, Vanuatu, Micronesia, Solomon Islands, Nauru, Kiribati, Tuvalu, Marshall Islands, and Papua New Guinea
- **Period:** 2010-2020

### Key Visualizations

- **Horizontal Bar Chart** - Total cumulative E-Government index per country, ranked from lowest (Fiji: 1,052.3) to highest (Papua New Guinea: 1,824.2)
- **Line Chart** - Observation value trends over time per country, revealing fluctuations and growth patterns across the decade
- **Pie Chart** - Distribution of rank categories: 51.28% in rank 101–150, 47.43% in rank 151+, and only 1.29% in the Top 50

### Key Findings

- **Papua New Guinea** recorded the highest total E-Government index (1,824.2) among all Pacific nations observed
- **Fiji** ranked lowest in cumulative index (1,052.3), indicating relatively lower digital government adoption
- The majority of Pacific nations (over 98%) fall outside the global Top 50, highlighting significant room for digital government improvement in the region
- Index values show notable fluctuations year-over-year, with most countries peaking around 2018–2020

### Tools Used

- **Tableau** - dashboard design, interactive filters, and all visualizations
- **Microsoft Excel** - data preprocessing and cleaning
